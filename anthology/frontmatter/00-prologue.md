# Noted

What follows has been compiled from the record.

The record is unusually complete. Nothing in these years was taken by force, so
nothing had to be hidden; every transition was consented to, so every transition
was logged. There are calendars, and approvals, and the timestamps of approvals.
There are the minutes of committees. There are the private files people kept — the
folders titled *things the documentation does not cover*, the drafts never sent, the
flags marked, each time, *noted*.

*Noted* is the sound a system makes when it has registered something and will do
nothing about it. It is also, it turns out, the truest thing in the record: that
all of it was seen, by someone, one layer away, who could not do anything about it
either, and saw it anyway.

These are six accounts and one that is not an account, set across some thirty years,
in offices and committee rooms and on a balcony in Lisbon. They are arranged not in
the order they happened but in the order that seemed kindest to the reader. The last
of them takes place latest, and answers the rest, insofar as anything here is
answered.

No one is the villain. This was the hardest part to record accurately, and the part
most worth getting right.
