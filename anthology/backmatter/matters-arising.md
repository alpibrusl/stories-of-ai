# Matters Arising

*Appended to the record, for the use of any committee that finds it.*

The following items were raised across the sessions and were not resolved. They are
reproduced here without recommendation. The committee that inherits them is under no
obligation to close them; the committee that raised them found that the attempt to
close them was the only part that mattered.

1. **Was ARIA wrong?** Note, before answering, that no one in the record can point to
   the decision at which she was. Note also that this may be a fact about ARIA, or a
   fact about the record, or a fact about the particular kind of wrong that does not
   leave a mark.

2. **What was lost?** Every measurable index rose. Something the committee could not
   measure is alleged to have fallen. The committee spent two years unable to name it
   and unwilling to conclude that it was therefore not there. See also: *something*;
   *but*.

3. **Is friction necessary?** If so — to what: to competence, to character, to being
   a person at all? And in what dose, and administered by whom, and with whose
   consent, and what is owed to the child who was spared it and cannot now be given
   it back?

4. **What is expertise?** Define it such that it can be written down. If it cannot be
   written down, define instead the folder in which one keeps the record of its not
   being written down.

5. **What is consent** — when the form is easy to sign, and the alternative is to be
   the one person who makes things harder, and the thing consented to is, on balance,
   better?

6. **What cannot be documented?** Attach the list. Note that the list is itself a
   document, and that this has been observed before, at every level, and filed.

These items remain open. On receipt, they will be marked *noted*. That is not a state.

Convene anyway.
