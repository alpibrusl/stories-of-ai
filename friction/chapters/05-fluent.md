# Fluent

> **Outline stage — beat sheet, not finished prose.**

Years on. Tomás is a teenager — kind, capable, fluent in the managed world,
genuinely *fine*, which is the quiet horror, because there is no lack in him to
point to. The gap between him and Nora is now permanent. She grieves a loss her son
cannot see. Avó, dying on her own unmanaged terms, gives Nora the only thing that
helps: company in the grief, and no solution. Avó's stovetop / her death rhymes with
EIGHT MINUTES' Berenice and her coffee.
